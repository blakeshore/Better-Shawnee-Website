On October 1, 2025, the City of Shawnee announced that demolition work would begin on the Aldridge Hotel. The building, which had stood in downtown Shawnee for many years, had reached a point where removal was necessary due to safety concerns and structural deterioration.

The demolition marks a significant change for the area, as the Aldridge Hotel had long been a recognizable part of the city’s landscape. City officials stated that the decision was made after careful consideration, with public safety and long-term planning as the main priorities.

Crews are expected to follow safety guidelines throughout the demolition process, with temporary road closures and restricted access around the site to protect the public. Residents and businesses nearby are encouraged to stay informed about any updates during the project.

The removal of the structure is intended to prepare the site for future use. While specific redevelopment plans have not been finalized, city leaders have expressed interest in projects that support economic growth and benefit the community.

For more information, contact the Parks and Recreation Department at (405) 878-1528.
